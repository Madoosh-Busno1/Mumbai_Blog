Heritage · Conservation
INTACH (Indian National Trust for Art and Cultural Heritage)

Founded in 1984, INTACH is India's largest heritage conservation organisation, with chapters across the country including Mumbai. It has led or supported the restoration of hundreds of monuments and historic precincts that fall outside government protection, including conservation work in Khotachiwadi and other sites featured on this blog.

Animal Welfare
Welfare of Stray Dogs (WSD)

A Mumbai-based trust set up in 1985 to stop the mass culling of street dogs by the municipal corporation. WSD now runs sterilisation, vaccination, on-site first aid, and adoption programmes across the city, all offered free of charge, and has directly helped well over a hundred thousand street dogs.

Education
The Akanksha Foundation

Founded in Mumbai in 1991 by Shaheen Mistri, Akanksha runs English-medium schools in partnership with municipal authorities across Mumbai, Pune, and Nagpur, working with children from low-income communities from kindergarten through to their first jobs. It now reaches more than 13,000 students through a network of 27 schools.

Education · Urban Poverty
Door Step School

Established in Mumbai in 1989, Door Step School brings education directly to children living in slums, on construction sites, and on pavements, through community-based classes, mobile "school on wheels" buses, and learning centres. It has reached well over a million children across Mumbai and Pune since its founding.

Elderly Care
Dignity Foundation

Headquartered in Byculla and founded in 1995, Dignity Foundation works to change how India thinks about ageing. It runs day-care and dementia-care centres, a loneliness mitigation programme, and "Chai Masti" social centres where senior citizens gather, a cause with particular relevance to Mumbai's ageing Parsi and East Indian communities.

Homelessness · Destitution
Koshish (Tata Institute of Social Sciences)

A field action project of TISS Mumbai, started in 2006 to challenge the law that criminalises begging and destitution. Koshish works directly with homeless communities on Mumbai's streets, helps people held in beggars' homes get released and resettled, and pushes for policy reform that treats poverty as a circumstance rather than a crime.

About This Blog

I'm Medhansh Sharma, a Mumbaikar fascinated by the layers of history that lie beneath the city's relentless present. In a metropolis that never stops building, it's easy to walk past a crumbling Portuguese chapel or a colonial-era cotton exchange without a second glance.

This blog is my attempt to slow down and look. Each post is a deep dive into a single place: its origins, the forces that shaped it, the people who built and inhabited it, and what it means today. I've been wandering Mumbai's streets, libraries, and archives for years, and here is where I share what I find.

The city was born from seven islands stitched together by land reclamation, colonialism, and sheer ambition. Every neighbourhood is a palimpsest. Pull back the surface and you find Kolis, Portuguese traders, British administrators, Parsi merchants, mill workers, and freedom fighters, all coexisting in the geological strata of a single place.
